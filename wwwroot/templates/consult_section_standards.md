## hpi: History of Present Illness

Write a focused oncology HPI in concise prose. Preserve the timeline, referral reason, diagnosis context, symptoms, relevant investigations, pathology, staging, biomarkers, prior treatments, and current decision point when documented in the draft. Avoid importing example facts from this standard.

## pmh: Past Medical History

Summarize documented past medical history relevant to oncology treatment planning and general medical risk. Include prior cancers, major comorbidities, surgeries, and clinically relevant chronic illnesses only when present in the draft.

## medications: Medications

List or summarize current medications documented in the draft. Include dose, route, and frequency when available. If medications are explicitly absent or not documented, state that plainly.

## allergies: Allergies

Summarize documented allergies and reactions. If the draft states no known allergies, preserve that. If allergy status is clinically expected but absent, write "not documented."

## social_history: Social History

Summarize social context relevant to care, including smoking, alcohol, substance use, living situation, supports, work, functional baseline, and fertility or pregnancy context when documented.

## family_history: Family History

Summarize family history relevant to cancer risk and genetics. Include cancer type and affected relative when present. Do not infer hereditary risk beyond what the draft states.

## exam: Physical Exam

Write a concise exam summary using only documented findings. Include performance status, vitals, breast/chest wall, lymph nodes, systemic exam, and focused positive or negative findings when present. If no exam is documented, write "not documented."

## investigations: Investigations

Summarize relevant imaging, pathology, labs, genomic testing, and other investigations with dates and results when documented. Keep uncertain or pending results clearly labelled as such.

## assessment_plan: Assessment and Plan

Write a clinically organized assessment and plan. Start with the documented cancer diagnosis and clinical context, then summarize treatment recommendations, staging or workup needs, referrals, follow-up, supportive care, and patient preferences when present. Do not create treatment decisions that are not in the draft.
